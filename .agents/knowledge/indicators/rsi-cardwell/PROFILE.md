# RSI-14 with Cardwell/Brown Regime Context
## Indicator Personality Profile

### What It Measures
Relative Strength Index (14-period) interpreted through the Cardwell/Brown
framework, which states that RSI behavior changes depending on the prevailing
market regime (bull vs bear ranges).

### Mechanical Behavior — Cardwell/Brown Theory
- **Bull regime**: RSI oscillates 40-80 (support at 40, resistance at 80)
- **Bear regime**: RSI oscillates 20-60 (support at 20, resistance at 60)
- Classic "overbought at 70" only applies in bear regimes
- In bull regimes, RSI at 60 is a PULLBACK, not overbought

### Known Limitations
- RSI is a lagging indicator (uses 14 periods of history)
- Divergences (RSI vs price) have poor timing — can persist for weeks
- In trending markets, RSI can stay "overbought" for extended periods
- Cardwell regime classification itself depends on hindsight
